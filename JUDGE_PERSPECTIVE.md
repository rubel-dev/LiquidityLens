## What 90% of Teams Will Do (and Why They Won't Win)

- Build **3 separate balance cards** and call it "unified view"

- Hardcode Bengali strings and call it "multilingual support"

## What Judges Actually Want to See for Top 3

---

### 🥇 1. The "Deceptive Total" Moment

This is the **core insight** of the entire problem and most teams will miss it visually.

Show this in the first 30 seconds of your demo:

```
Total Balance: ৳ 82,000  ✅  (looks healthy)

  bKash:   ৳  1,200  🔴  (almost empty — runs out in 38 min)
  Nagad:   ৳ 45,000  ✅
  Rocket:  ৳ 35,800  ✅
  Cash:    ৳    ---  🔴  (data missing)
```

The aggregate is GREEN. The agent thinks they're fine. They're not.
**That one visual will make judges remember your team.**

---

### 🥇 2. Tell the Eid Story — Don't Just Show Features

Don't open with "here is our dashboard". Open like this:

> *"It's 4:47 PM. Eid is tomorrow. An agent in Mirpur is serving a line of 12 customers.
> He has no idea his bKash balance runs out in 38 minutes.
> His field officer has no idea either.
> Let us show you what happens — with and without our system."*

Judges are human. Narrative makes them feel the problem.
**Teams that make judges feel something win.**

---

### 🥇 3. Live Bengali Alert Generated by AI in Real Time

Don't hardcode Bengali. During the demo, trigger an alert and show Claude generating the Bengali message **live.** Show the API call happening. Judges will notice.

```
[Alert triggered] → [Calling AI...] → [Bengali message appears]

"বর্তমান লেনদেনের ধারা অনুযায়ী ৩৮ মিনিটের মধ্যে
আপনার বিকাশ ব্যালেন্স শেষ হয়ে যেতে পারে..."
```

This is the difference between **"we used AI"** and **"we demonstrated AI working."**

---

### 🥇 4. Anomaly With a Story, Not Just a Flag

**What 90% of teams show:**
```
⚠️ Unusual activity detected.
```

**What top 3 teams show:**
```
⚠️ Requires Human Review — Velocity + Amount Pattern

  6 cash-out transactions in the last 12 minutes
  All between ৳4,900 – ৳5,050  (suspiciously clustered)
  From only 2 unique accounts  (account ratio: 0.33, normal: 0.85+)
  Confidence: 71%  |  Uncertainty: HIGH

  ℹ️  This COULD be normal Eid demand.
      Do NOT act without human review.
      Recommended: Field officer calls agent before next rebalance.
```

**Evidence + uncertainty + careful language + recommended action = the full package.**

---

### 🥇 5. Do Scenario C — The One Everyone Skips

Simulate turning off the Nagad mock API mid-demo. Show:

- Nagad card turns yellow → *"Data delayed (4 min 12 sec)"*
- Aggregate confidence drops to LOW
- Dashboard shows: *"Nagad balance estimate unreliable — recommendation withheld"*
- System keeps working for bKash and Rocket without crashing

**This single feature demonstrates more engineering maturity than any fancy ML model.**
Judges wrote it into the problem statement specifically because they want to see if teams handle
real-world messiness. Almost nobody will.

---

### 🥇 6. Show a False Positive Being Handled Correctly

Show a normal salary-day spike that the system does **NOT** flag as anomaly. Say:

> *"Our system saw 14 cash-outs in 20 minutes — it did NOT flag this as unusual
> because it's the 1st of the month, which matches historical salary-day patterns
> in our baseline."*

This shows you thought about fairness and false positives — which is explicitly in the
evaluation criteria and which almost no team will demonstrate.

---

### 🥇 7. Full Coordination Lifecycle in One Click-Through

Don't just show an alert sitting in a list. During the demo, click through the entire lifecycle live:

```
Alert fires
  → Field Officer sees it on Ops Dashboard
  → Clicks "Acknowledge"  (timestamp recorded)
  → Assigns to Risk Analyst with a note
  → Risk Analyst adds case note: "Pattern reviewed, likely Eid demand"
  → Marks as Resolved
  → Alert history shows full audit trail
```

**Every step visible. Every step clickable. Every step logged.**
That's a coordination workflow — not a notification bell.

---

### 🥇 8. Real Metrics With Real Numbers

Don't say *"our system is accurate."* Say:

| Metric | Your Number |
|--------|------------|
| Shortage detection lead time | **43 minutes** before depletion (avg across 50 test cases) |
| Anomaly precision | **87%** on injected test scenarios |
| False positive rate on Eid/salary-day spikes | **11%** |
| Alert explanation coverage | **100%** of HIGH alerts have reason + evidence + Bengali message |
| API p95 latency | **< 200ms** for `/balances` endpoint |

Put these on one slide. **Judges remember numbers.**

> These are example numbers — replace with your actual measured values.

## The 3-Second Rule

Judges form **80% of their opinion in the first 3 minutes** of your demo.
Make sure those 3 minutes have:

| Time | What to Show |
|------|-------------|
| 0:00 – 0:30 | The Eid story hook |
| 0:30 – 1:00 | The deceptive total visual |
| 1:00 – 2:00 | One alert firing with live Bengali AI message |

Everything else is supporting detail.

## What Will Lose You Points (Even With Good Code)

- ❌ Using the word "fraud" anywhere in the UI
- ❌ Merging provider balances without clear separation
- ❌ No Bengali support (it's recommended, most teams skip it)
- ❌ Demo using hardcoded/frozen data that doesn't change
- ❌ Vague metrics ("fast", "accurate", "efficient")
- ❌ No handling of missing or late provider data
- ❌ Alert that fires but has no owner, no next step, no resolution
- ❌ Architecture diagram with no provider boundary separation
